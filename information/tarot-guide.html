Basic Tarot Guide

Welcome to the world of tarot—a mystical tool for self-reflection, guidance, and insight. Whether you're using a free tarot card generator or shuffling a physical deck, the magic lies in interpretation. This guide will introduce you to the foundational knowledge of tarot cards and how to begin your own readings.

1. What Is Tarot?

Tarot is a symbolic system made up of 78 cards—divided into the Major and Minor Arcana—used to gain insight into the past, present, and future. Each card carries a unique energy and meaning that reflects life's universal themes.

2. The Structure of a Tarot Deck

A standard tarot deck includes:

  • 22 Major Arcana: Represent life’s karmic and spiritual lessons (e.g., The Fool, The Lovers, The Tower).
  • 56 Minor Arcana: Represent day-to-day events, split into four suits—Cups, Pentacles, Swords, and Wands.

3. How to Read Tarot

Start by asking a clear question. Draw cards using your intuition or a random tarot card generator. Common spreads include:

  • Single Card Draw: Ideal for quick daily guidance.
  • 3 Card Spread: Covers Past – Present – Future or Situation – Action – Outcome.
  • Celtic Cross: A more detailed spread for deep inquiries.

4. Understanding Card Meanings

Every card has a general meaning that shifts with context and position. Reversed cards (upside-down) often indicate blocked or internalized energy. Our tarot card generator with meaning includes upright and reversed interpretations for each draw.

5. Practice with Intuition

Tarot isn't about predicting the future with certainty—it’s about connecting to your subconscious. Use our AI tarot card generator for intuitive practice. Meditate on the imagery, note your emotions, and allow interpretations to surface naturally.
